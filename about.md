Sunday is not an app. It is not an assistant. It is a shell — rebuilt from the human outward — that sits on top of macOS the way Aqua sits on top of Darwin. You speak or type what you need. Sunday does it. The screen shows you that it happened.

This journal is the build log, written in the open. Decisions, wrong turns, the things that worked the second time. It is not a roadmap and not a marketing channel. It is what one developer thought on a given week, recorded so the work is legible to anyone who finds it later — including the person writing it.

The technical reality is simple. Native macOS, Swift and SwiftUI, no cross-platform compromises. Apple Foundation Models on the Neural Engine for fast structured output, a local LLM via Ollama for deeper reasoning. SwiftData and sqlite-vec for memory. SFSpeechRecognizer on-device for voice. Nothing leaves the machine.
